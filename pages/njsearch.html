How to Use Bridge Navigator
1
Search by Bridge or Structure
Search by structure number or bridge name. Structure numbers display in the XXXX-XXX format, and searches work with or without the dash.
2
Use the Map and Street View
Selecting a bridge centers the map on its NBI location. Use Google Maps or Street View from the bridge header when you need to open the location outside this page.
3
Share Bridge Details
Use Share to send the key bridge information. Desktop copies the structure number, bridge name, route, milepost, and Google Maps pin link. Mobile opens an SMS message with the same text ready to send.
Structure Route Milepost
4
Copy Data Fields
Click or tap the bridge name or any detail field under the map to copy only the field value. For example, the Route field copies Route 202, not the field label.
5
Find Near Me and Saved Items
Find Near Me uses your current GPS location to detect nearby structures. Save important bridges with the star button, then use Show Saved to bring them back quickly.
