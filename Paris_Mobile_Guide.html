Heat Survival
Rule for Friday/Saturday: every route should have an indoor reset. Carry water, use bathrooms when you have them, and do not save all hydration for later.

Best AC/Bathroom Anchors

Hotel, Le Bon Marché, La Grande Épicerie, Cité de l’Architecture, BHV, La Samaritaine, major cafés/brasseries, and museums.

Water Strategy

Buy a cold bottle whenever you enter a pharmacy/food hall/Monoprix. In cafés, order water with espresso instead of waiting until you feel cooked.

When to Cut a Walk

If the sun feels harsh, skip the scenic detour and go straight to the indoor stop or meal. The Paris vibe is better when you are not overheated.

Storm Strategy

If storms show up Saturday/Sunday evening, switch to a nearby brasserie, department store, hotel-side meal, or Ground Control rather than crossing town.

Wife-Approved Extras Not Forced Into the Main Plan

Use these only as swaps, not chores. The goal is French vibes and beauty without wrecking the heat plan.

Route-Perfect Adds

Shakespeare & Company, Marais jewelry browsing, wine terrace, baguette/croissant moments, Eiffel sitting time, and hotel pool are already worked into the days.

Pretty Short Adds

Jardin du Palais-Royal, Musée Rodin, Parc Monceau, and Caveau de la Huchette are now optional add-ons before hotel fallback.

Big Swaps Only

Saint-Ouen Flea Market, Maison Fleuret class, and Monet’s Garden/Giverny are not casual add-ons. Use them only if you intentionally replace a large chunk of the plan.

La Roche-Posay Cicaplast Baume B5+

Target price: ~€11–13 / 100ml

Why buy: Safest rescue balm: dry patches, irritation, shaving irritation, post-sun dryness, heat/sweat rubbing.

How to recognize it: White tube, blue square, vertical Cicaplast Baume B5+ text.

Caution: Can feel heavy on oily faces during hot weather.

Product/SourceCityPharma Pin
La Roche-Posay Anthelios UVMune 400 SPF50+

Target price: often ~€15–20; verify shelf

Why buy: Big Paris heat purchase: strong modern European UVA protection, light face texture, travel friendly.

How to recognize it: Flat white bottle, orange Anthelios/UVMune 400 label, 50+.

Caution: Buy enough to reapply. If oily, look for Oil Control/green band.

Bioderma Sensibio / Créaline H2O Micellar Water

Target price: ~€7–10 / 500ml; 2-pack often deal

Why buy: Easy French pharmacy classic for makeup/sunscreen removal and sensitive skin cleansing.

How to recognize it: Clear bottle, pink cap/label, Sensibio H2O or Créaline H2O.

Caution: Heavy bottle; buy only if luggage space works.

Klorane Dry Shampoo

Target price: varies by size

Why buy: Very practical travel/hair refresh item and often cheaper in France than the US.

How to recognize it: Aerosol can; Klorane branding; oat milk versions are common.

A313 Pommade

Target price: ~€9–12 / 50g

Why buy: Cult French pharmacy vitamin A ointment. Buy it only if you specifically wanted the A313/retinoid-style item for later, not as a heat-trip experiment.

How to recognize it: Blue-and-white box/tube with large A313 text.

Caution: Medication. Ask the pharmacist and read the leaflet. Do not start it during the trip, do not use like casual retinol, and avoid damaged/burned skin, large areas, eyes, or mucous membranes.

Product/SourceCityPharma Pin
Biafine Emulsion

Target price: ~€5–7 / 93g

Why buy: Classic French burn/sunburn cream for superficial, localized burns or sunburns. Useful as a small just-in-case item if you are worried about the heat/sun.

How to recognize it: White box/tube with large green BIAFINE lettering.

Caution: Not sunscreen and not a daily moisturizer. Ask the pharmacist; get medical help for blistering, large burns, infected/bleeding wounds, or burns on sensitive areas.

Product/SourceCityPharma Pin
Do not start every active at once

For the trip: use sunscreen, barrier cream, lip balm, and maybe micellar water. Save new actives like A313 and new serums/oils for after travel unless you already know your skin tolerates them.

Ticket math

Pay-per-ride is still the best fit. Regular Metro/RER rides are about €2.55 each; airport ticket to CDG is about €14. Paris Visite is not worth it for this plan. Weekly Navigo only makes sense if you take 13+ rides before Sunday night, which your relaxed heat-proof plan probably won’t.
